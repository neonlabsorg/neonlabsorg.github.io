How to Swap SPL Tokens

Goal#

Transfer SPL custom tokens from the Solana blockchain to the ERC-20 account balance in Neon EVM via the Neon bridge.

Conditions:

  • You have an account in Neon EVM.
  • The SPL token can be SOL, ETH, USDT, or USDC.
  • The Neon Bridge application is used as a browser.

Before you begin#

  • Understand the following:
    • What is the Solana network.
    • What is the Metamask.
    • What is the Phantom.
  • Both Metamask and Phantom must be installed on your device and can be accessed from the browser you are using.
  • Learn the guide "How to Connect Metamask to Solana's Network".

Steps#

The application uses Metamask and Phantom ‚Äî two popular non-custodial browser based wallets. This pair of wallets gives users the ability to transfer SPL tokens to Neon EVM.

Connect your wallet#

Click onto connect wallet in the top right hand side of the screen.

Step 1. Open the Neon Bridge application#

The NeonSwap tool allows any user to trade one token for another. You can see how simple it is. Go to the page ... . You can access this page by clicking the swap link (—É–∫–∞–∑–∞—Ç—å —Å—Å—ã–ª–∫—É –Ω–∞ –≥–ª–∞–≤–Ω–æ–π —Å—Ç—Ä–∞–Ω–∏—Ü–µ) in the upper left hand area of the page.

Before you can make a trade you need to connect your wallet. Click Connect Wallet on the trade screen.

From the list appeared on the dropdown widow You have to select the Metamask (recommended) wallet. Select the account you want to connect to.

After connecting your wallet you can begin setting the trade. In the from box click the dropdown arrow on the right hand site. This will open up a menu where you can select token for swapping. You can type the name of the token in the search box.

If the token name you specified is not in the list, you can paste the token's address if you know what it is. You can get a token's address from a namber of places including etherscan.com and coingecko.com.

At the bottom you can change the list of tokens that are available for traning. Now in the box you can select the token to buy (to swap - ?)

In this example we will be buying a token called SOL (–Ω–∞ –∫–∞—Ä—Ç–∏–Ω–∫–µ - AAVE), which is the token for the borrowing and lending platform SOL.

Enter the quantity you want to sell. Before you can send this trade by clicking Swap you need to understand a few things about it:

  • Minimum received - the minimum amount of SOL you will receive if the trade goes through. The trade will fail if price diverges too much during the transaction also known as slippage.
  • Price impact tells you how much your trade will affect this token's price. This value can be seen as aloss you will take on the trade. The closer this number is to zero the better.
  • Liquidity Provider Fee - the fee you will make this trade. Uniswap charges a 0.3 percent fee on the total amount of the trade. This fee is expressed in the token you are selling (In this case ETH).

Once you are ready to perform the trade click Swap.

A confirmation window will pop up with details of the trade. Click Confirm Swap. This will open up a MetaMask window where you can confirm the transaction. You can see estimate of how much gas you will pay

It should encourage you to check the transaction speed and set it to either average or fas. A faster transaction will cost more but it will also reduce the chances of your transaction failing due to slippage. Click Confirm to send the transaction.

The transaction is complete which means you have successfully traded your ETH for SOL.

Step 10. Additional settings for trading#

Click the cogwheel icon. The window Transaction Settings will pop-up on the screen to set up the advanced trade:

Transaction settings:

  • Slippage tolerance ‚Äî the allowable amount of price change. It means that the order is fulfilled at the price different from what you expected. If it exceeds the tolerance level selected, the transaction will not go through. If you are trading during high volatility, you may want to increase this value. Increasing the transaction speed can help you to reduce the chances of high slippage.
  • Transaction deadline ‚Äî allows the transaction to automatically cancel if it is taking too long to process.
  • Toggle Expert Mode ‚Äî disables the confirmation screen and removes slippage limits. It is not encouraged you to enable this mode unless you fully understand the risks.
  • Disable Multihops ‚Äî disables complex conversion paths. For example, if there is not enough liquidity in the "A->B" token pair, then the algorithm can involve other tokens to find the "A->C->B" intermediate route and make an exchange for you. This action requires more network fees, because several smart contracts are involved.
